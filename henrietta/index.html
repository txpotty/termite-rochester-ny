Comprehensive Termite Inspection and Assessment

The first crucial step in effective termite control is a detailed inspection. Our technicians are highly skilled at identifying the subtle signs of termite activity that may be missed by the untrained eye. During our inspection, we meticulously examine:

Interior and Exterior Structures

We inspect basements, crawl spaces, attics, walls, and all accessible wood elements within your property. Identifying Termite Species

Knowing the specific type of termite involved is essential for selecting the most effective treatment. In Henrietta, the most common culprits are subterranean termites, but drywood termites and dampwood termites can also pose threats. Our technicians are experts in identifying different species based on their physical characteristics, mud tubes, fecal pellets (frass), and other telltale signs. Accurate identification is key to developing a targeted treatment plan that addresses the specific biology and behavior of the invading species. For example, the treatment for subterranean termites, which build mud tubes for transportation, differs significantly from the treatment for drywood termites, which infest wood directly without soil contact. Our expertise in distinguishing between these species ensures we apply the most appropriate and effective control methods for your specific situation. Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer an alternative or complementary approach to liquid treatments, particularly for certain types of infestations or in situations where liquid application is challenging. These systems involve placing bait stations in the ground around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting insect growth regulator that termites consume and carry back to their colony, eventually leading to the decline and elimination of the entire colony. Baiting systems are environmentally friendly and require ongoing monitoring and replenishment to be effective. Our technicians are trained in the proper placement and maintenance of these systems, ensuring continuous protection. Baiting systems are particularly effective when targeting large or dispersed colonies, as they exploit the termites' foraging behavior to deliver the control agent directly to the heart of the colony. They are also a good option for properties with wells or groundwater concerns, as they minimize the use of liquid products in the soil. The monitoring process is crucial with baiting systems, requiring regular inspection of the stations to detect termite activity and replenish the bait as needed. Our service includes ongoing monitoring and maintenance of the baiting system to ensure its continued effectiveness in intercepting and eliminating termite colonies. The slow-acting nature of the bait allows termites to share the active ingredient with their nest mates, leading to a cascading effect that ultimately eliminates the entire colony, including the reproductives. This is a significant advantage as it targets the source of the infestation, not just the foraging termites. The discrete placement of the bait stations minimizes visual impact on your landscaping, offering a less intrusive treatment option compared to some liquid applications. We work with you to determine if a termite baiting system is the right solution for your property based on the type of infestation, the structure of your home, and your personal preferences. Preventative Measures and Ongoing Monitoring

Eliminating an existing termite infestation is only part of the solution. Effective termite control also involves implementing preventative measures and establishing ongoing monitoring to minimize the risk of future problems. We provide valuable advice and services to help keep your property termite-free in the long term.

Removing Termite Attractants

Simple steps can significantly reduce the attractiveness of your property to termites. This includes removing wood debris, old lumber, and excess cellulose materials from around your foundation. Ensuring proper drainage away from your foundation and repairing leaky pipes or faucets can also help, as termites are attracted to moisture. We can provide guidance on these and other preventative measures you can take to make your property less inviting to termites. Clearing brush and vegetation from the perimeter of your home helps to create a physical barrier and reduces areas where termites can hide and build mud tubes unnoticed. Storing firewood at least 20 feet away from your home and elevated off the ground is another important preventative measure, as firewood is a common source of termite infestations. Addressing any areas of standing water or excessive moisture around your foundation is crucial, as termites require moisture to survive and thrive. We will walk you through an assessment of your property to identify potential termite attractants and provide actionable recommendations for their removal or mitigation. Simple landscaping adjustments, such as sloping the ground away from your foundation and ensuring gutters and downspouts direct water away from the house, can make a significant difference in preventing termite infestations. Regularly checking for and repairing any cracks or gaps in your foundation and exterior walls can also prevent termites from gaining easy access to your home. Preventing direct wood-to-soil contact is paramount, which can be achieved by ensuring that wooden components of your home, such as siding and trim, are not in direct contact with the soil. We can provide detailed guidance on how to implement these preventative landscaping and maintenance practices to make your property less susceptible to termite infestations.
